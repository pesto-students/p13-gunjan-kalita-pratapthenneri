TIRUPATI

Location

Tirupati is a city in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. Its Sri Venkateswara Temple sits atop one of the the 7 peaks of Tirumala Hills, attracting scores of Hindu pilgrims. Sri Venkateswara National Park, home to the temple, also contains the Sri Venkateswara Zoological Park with lions and primates. Nearby, next to a waterfall and cave believed to be sacred, Sri Kapileswara Swamy Temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

Tirupati is a city in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India. It's famous for the important Hindu temple called Tirumala Venkateshwara Temple, as well as other historic temples. The city is the administrative headquarters of the Tirupati district.

Tirupati is located about 150 km from Chennai, 250 km from Bangalore, and 406 km from Amaravati. It is one of the eight Svayam vyakta kshetras, special temples dedicated to the deity Vishnu.

The city has a growing population, with around 459,985 people in 2011 and about 1,004,615 people in 2021. It's the 7th most urbanized city in the state.

Tirupati received recognition as the "Best Heritage City" by India's Ministry of Tourism in 2012-2013. Additionally, it has been selected as one of the hundred cities to be developed as a smart city under the Smart Cities Mission by the Government of India.

About

Climate

Tirupati has a tropical wet and dry climate designated as Aw (dry "winter") under the koppen climate classification system. During the dry season, which lasts from January to May, average minimum temperatures are between 18 and 28 °C (64.4 and 82.4 °F). The dry season is followed by the rainy season, which lasts from June until the end of December. The city experiences heavy rainfall in November during the northeast monsoon season. The highest rainfall in 24 hours (219 millimetres or 8.62 inches) was on 16 November 2015, during the 2015 South India floods. Cyclones commonly hit the Coast of Nellore and bring heavy rain to the city.

Ancient history

Tirupati has a long and rich history. It was developed by the Pallava Kings from the 6th century onwards. During the 11th century, it became a significant Vaishnava center under the influence of Ramanujacharya, spreading Srivaishnavism to other parts of Andhra Desa. The city survived Muslim invasions by agreeing to pay Jizya to the Muslims. In the early 1300s, during the Muslim invasion of South India, the deity of Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, Srirangam, was brought to Tirupati for safekeeping. For most of the medieval era, Tirupati was part of the Vijayanagara Empire until the 17th century. The Vijayanagara rulers, such as Krishna Deva Raya and Achyuta Deva Raya, contributed considerable resources and wealth to the city. Tirupati boasts numerous historical temples, with the Venkateshvara Temple being one of the most prominent. This temple bears 1,150 inscriptions in Sanskrit, Telugu, Kannada, and Tamil languages. These inscriptions highlight the contributions of various dynasties like Pallava, Chola, Pandya, Saluva, Achyuta Deva Raya, Sadasiva Raya, and Aravidu. During the 15th century, Sri Tallapaka Annamacharya, a great composer, sung many songs praising Tirupati's divinity. He compared the town to heaven on earth, including its rocks, streams, trees, and animals. One example of such a song is:

Kattedura Vaikuntamu Kaanachaina Konda

Thettalaya Mahimale Tirumala Konda ||

Vedamulae Silalai Velasinadi Konda

Yedesa Punya Rasule Yerulainadi Konda

Gadili Brahmadi Lokamula Konalu

Konda Sree Devudundeti Seshadri Konda ||

Tirumala is a heavenly place with incredible powers. It is said that the Vedas themselves have taken the form of rocks there. Holy water flows as streams on Tirumala, and its peaks are considered to be like the realms of the gods, including Brahmaloka. Srinivasa, a deity, resides on Seshadri hill.

Until the year 1500, there were no human settlements in Lower Tirupati. The importance of Upper Tirupati grew, and a village was established at the present-day Kapilatheertham Road area called "kotturu." Later, the village was moved closer to the Govindarajaswamy Temple, which was consecrated around 1130 CE. Over time, the village expanded around the Govindaraja Swamy Temple and became the city as it is now. It has become a popular tourist destination.
